How to find low-competition keywords for a new website

Most keyword research advice is written for established sites with domain authority scores above 40. If your site is under 12 months old with fewer than 20 published articles, that advice does not apply to you.

Low-competition keywords for new websites share four characteristics: they are four or more words long, they target a specific audience segment rather than a broad topic, the current top results include at least one piece of community content like a Reddit thread or Quora answer, and the ranking pages were published more than 18 months ago without being updated.

This free keyword research tool filters every keyword it generates through those four criteria automatically. The Quick Win flags indicate keywords where a site with low domain authority has a realistic path to page one within 60 to 90 days.

What makes a keyword rankable for a solo founder?

A keyword difficulty score measures the average domain authority of pages currently ranking — not whether your specific site can rank. The only reliable way to assess rankability is a manual SERP audit: open an incognito window, search the keyword, and scan the top ten results for weak or outdated content.
